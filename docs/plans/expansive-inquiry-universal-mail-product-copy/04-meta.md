## System Diagnosis

Scope identified the central split: the current product surface is trying to sell both inbox safety and a broader agent platform. Logic clarified that the landing page sequence is the main defect, not the existence of the proof layer. Mythos reframed the product from "automation" to "accountable custodian." Bridge supplied concrete external structures where cleanup and preservation coexist.

The lenses agree on one system-level diagnosis: the product already has credible machinery, but its first-contact language makes the buyer climb the machinery before understanding the rescue.

## Feedback Loop

Regenerate the product story in this order:

1. Primitive claim
   - One sentence.
   - No architecture nouns.
   - Must be understandable by someone who only knows their inbox is a mess.

2. Buyer consequence
   - Name the emails people are afraid to lose.
   - Name the bad outcome the product prevents.
   - Avoid generic "productivity" framing.

3. Safety demonstration
   - Show a sender or message.
   - Mark it as safe to move, never move, or needs review.
   - Use the live demo as evidence of behavior.

4. Proof translation
   - Human phrase first: "You get a receipt."
   - Technical phrase second: "signed and re-derivable."

5. Technical legitimacy
   - Provider support.
   - Shared rules engine.
   - Fail-closed gate.
   - Audit receipt.
   - MCP/ACP/agent surfaces only after the buyer understands the core product.

Prune:

- "Provable restraint" as the first eyebrow.
- "Eisenhower priority tiers" from the hero.
- "MCP + ACP" from first-screen badges for nontechnical buyers.
- "Compliance Evidence Pack" until after the plain receipt concept lands.

Re-run:

- The hero copy.
- The demo framing.
- The pricing blurbs.
- The "agents" section as either advanced proof or a separate product lane.

## Three Adaptations

1. Add a "third-grade first pass" requirement to every buyer-facing sentence: if the plain version does not exist, the technical version cannot ship.

2. Treat proof as layered disclosure. The first layer reassures; the second layer substantiates; the third layer documents.

3. Split product thinking from platform thinking. The platform can remain architecturally broad, but each launch surface needs one buyer job.
