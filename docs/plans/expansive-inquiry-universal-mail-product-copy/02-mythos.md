## Five Framings

1. The locked document box inside the messy garage.
2. The bouncer who refuses to throw out anyone with a real invitation.
3. The night clerk who writes down every movement in the building.
4. The floodgate that lets rainwater drain but keeps the house sealed.
5. The librarian who will not discard a book unless the catalog still knows what happened.

## Stories

- The locked document box inside the messy garage
  - The buyer's inbox is not just clutter. It is a garage where tax papers, court letters, bank warnings, receipts, and old family notes are buried under cardboard and junk mail.
  - The product does not promise to make the garage beautiful first. It promises to find the locked box, protect it, and then clear the junk around it.
  - Archetypal pattern: sanctuary inside chaos.

- The bouncer who refuses to throw out anyone with a real invitation
  - Normal automation is an overeager cleaner. It moves fast and can make consequential mistakes.
  - This product is a bouncer with a strict guest list: courts, banks, government domains, account senders, and other protected sources are not removed just because they look inconvenient.
  - Archetypal pattern: guardian at the threshold.

- The night clerk who writes down every movement in the building
  - The receipt is not decoration. It is the quiet clerk who records what door opened, what stayed, what moved, and whether the rules were violated.
  - The buyer may not read the log every day, but its existence changes the feeling of the system: the machine is accountable.
  - Archetypal pattern: witness.

- The floodgate that lets rainwater drain but keeps the house sealed
  - Email clutter is not a pile; it is weather. It keeps arriving.
  - The product must drain the flow without opening the house to loss. It is not a one-time cleanup but a controlled channel.
  - Archetypal pattern: boundary against overwhelm.

- The librarian who will not discard a book unless the catalog still knows what happened
  - A library can remove duplicates and flyers because it has a catalog and policy.
  - This product gives the inbox that dignity: no silent disappearance, no mystery cleanup, no vague "AI handled it."
  - Archetypal pattern: custodian of memory.

## Archetypal Reading

The product's myth is not "the clever AI assistant." That frame is too common and too untrustworthy. Its better myth is "the accountable custodian": a quiet force that restores order while preserving what cannot be lost.

This matters for copy because "automation" suggests speed, while "custody" suggests care. The buyer feedback is asking the product to speak as a careful custodian before it speaks as an engineer.
