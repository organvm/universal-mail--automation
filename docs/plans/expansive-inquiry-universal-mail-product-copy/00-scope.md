## Core Inquiry

How should Universal Mail Automation translate its real technical strength into a first-contact product story that a nontechnical buyer understands immediately, while preserving the proof layer that makes the product more credible than a generic AI email cleaner?

## Essential vs. Peripheral

Essential:

- The primitive value: what the product does before any architecture words appear.
- The buyer panic: what makes someone care today, not someday.
- The safety claim: how to say "protected sender gate" without losing people.
- The proof claim: how to say "signed, re-derivable audit receipt" as human reassurance first and technical proof second.
- The launch shape: what can be shipped as one crisp product without waiting for the entire platform vision.
- The feedback from the screenshots: "clean and in depth" but "college level instead of 3rd grader."

Peripheral for this inquiry:

- Perfect pricing.
- Full MCP and ACP positioning.
- Complete backend architecture.
- Deep provider-by-provider technical documentation.
- Final brand naming.
- Whether the demo is the canonical backend.

## Hidden Ambiguities

The visible surface contains two products trying to speak at once:

1. Inbox Safety Cleaner: clean up email without losing court, bank, government, account, or client mail.
2. Agent Context Surface: let AI tools and agents coordinate across shared operational context.

The screenshots validate interest in both, but the strongest immediate buyer comprehension belongs to the first product. The second product is exciting but still needs its own simpler primitive.

The current page also confuses "proof" with "first sentence." Proof is powerful after the buyer understands the danger. It is alienating when it appears before the buyer has named the pain.
