## Topological Reading

Universal Mail Automation is currently shaped like an engineer's proof artifact with a product page wrapped around it. The underlying product shape is stronger than the surface language: it protects consequence-bearing email while cleaning the surrounding noise, then produces evidence of what happened.

The inquiry reveals a five-layer stack:

1. Fear: I might lose something important.
2. Safety: Important mail stays put.
3. Cleanup: Noise can move away.
4. Proof: I get a receipt showing what happened.
5. Architecture: shared rules, providers, gates, receipts, agents, commerce.

The current first screen starts at layers 4 and 5. The buyer feedback asks us to start at layers 1 and 2.

## Epistemic Signature

logic-heavy + proof-rich + buyer-language-thin + metaphorically recoverable + launchable-now

The system is not weak. The story is over-elevated. The strongest move is not to add complexity but to reduce the first-contact language until the buyer can repeat it back.

## Productive Contradictions

- Logic wants one clean launch product; the architecture wants to expose a broader platform.
  - Resolution: launch the inbox safety cleaner as the first buyer story; keep the agent platform as an advanced section or separate surface.

- Mythos wants "custodian" language; commerce wants "automation" language.
  - Resolution: use automation as mechanism, not identity. The identity is safe cleanup with proof.

- Proof is the differentiator, but proof also caused confusion when placed first.
  - Resolution: proof must be second-order. First say what stays safe; then say how the buyer can verify it.

## Three Meta-Patterns

1. Safety before speed.
   - The product should not sound like it is eager to touch everything. It should sound careful.

2. Receipt before compliance.
   - "Receipt" is the primitive. "Signed, re-derivable audit receipt" is the legitimacy layer.

3. Singular buyer job before platform breadth.
   - The first page should sell one job: clean this inbox safely.
