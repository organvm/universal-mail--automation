## Five Lines

1. What is the simplest buyer-visible job?
2. What pain makes that job urgent?
3. What proof changes this from a convenience tool into a trust product?
4. What should the landing page reveal first, second, and third?
5. Which product should be launched first if there are multiple product shapes?

## Recursive Tree

- Simplest buyer-visible job
  - Why does "automated triage across Gmail, Outlook, iCloud and IMAP" fail first-contact comprehension?
    - Because it explains how the machine is built before explaining what the buyer gets.
      - Therefore the first sentence should not name providers, rules engines, tiers, MCP, ACP, fail-closed gates, or receipts.
  - How should the primitive be stated?
    - "It cleans your inbox without losing important email."
      - Then define important with concrete examples: court, bank, government, account, client, family, health, travel.
  - What if the buyer is technical?
    - They can scroll into the proof layer.
      - The page should be progressive disclosure, not flattened simplification.

- Buyer pain
  - Why would someone pay instead of just using Gmail filters?
    - They are afraid of deleting or missing something that creates consequences.
      - The buyer is purchasing restraint, not automation speed.
  - How does the product map to consequence?
    - Mistaken archive, missed court notice, bank alert lost in promotions, account lockout email buried, client thread hidden.
      - The copy should name those concrete cases.
  - What if the inbox is only annoying, not dangerous?
    - Then the product becomes a nice-to-have.
      - The launch should target people whose inbox contains consequence-bearing messages.

- Proof layer
  - Why is "signed, re-derivable audit receipt" strong?
    - It creates after-the-fact evidence, not just a trust promise.
      - It distinguishes the product from black-box AI cleanup.
  - How should proof be translated?
    - "You get a receipt showing what stayed, what moved, and why."
      - Technical details can follow: signed, re-derivable, violation-checked.
  - What if proof sounds too legalistic?
    - Lead with everyday reassurance, then let compliance language appear for the buyer who needs it.
      - "Delete the junk. Keep the proof." bridges both.

- Page order
  - Why did the screenshot reviewer stall?
    - The page began with the proof vocabulary.
      - The user had to parse "provable restraint" before understanding "safe cleanup."
  - How should the order change?
    - Pain, simple promise, concrete examples, demo, proof, technical details, pricing.
      - The demo should demonstrate the primitive, not only the protected sender mechanism.
  - What if the current page is already credible?
    - Keep the credibility but move it down one layer.
      - The content is not wrong; the sequence is wrong.

- Launch choice
  - Why launch Inbox Safety Cleaner first?
    - It has a concrete buyer, live demo, pricing, tests, Cloudflare surface, and visible feedback.
      - It can become revenue before the larger agent-context product is fully narrated.
  - How should the AI coordination product be handled?
    - Treat it as a second lane or advanced feature story.
      - Do not let it dilute the first product's promise.
  - What if both are part of the same platform?
    - The platform can exist in architecture while the landing page sells one job.
      - Architecture can be plural; first-contact copy should be singular.

## Strongest Branch

The strongest branch is: the buyer is not buying automation. The buyer is buying safe deletion. Every copy and demo decision should be judged by whether it makes safe deletion obvious.
