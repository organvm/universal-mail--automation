## Three Meta-Patterns

1. The product is a boundary ritual.
   - It decides what may cross from inbox to archive.
   - It protects certain senders as taboo or sacred records.
   - It documents the crossing so the action is accountable.

2. The product's strongest value is negative capability.
   - It is valuable because of what it refuses to do.
   - "Never move protected mail" is more trust-building than "sorts across providers."
   - The buyer wants restraint more than magic.

3. The copy must descend before it ascends.
   - Start at the lowest abstraction: "important emails stay safe."
   - Then rise to proof: "you get a receipt."
   - Then rise to architecture: "shared rules engine, providers, signed receipts, MCP."
